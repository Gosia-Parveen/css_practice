DIV

1. Div as CONTAINER

The 'div' element is by default a block element, meaning that it takes all available width, and comes with line breaks before and after.

Here 'div' is used as a container .


2. GRIFFIN

Mythical creatures with the body of a lion and head and wings of an eagle.
They symbolize strength, courage, and wisdom and are known as guardians of treasures and sacred places.

NOTE: If you have a 'div' element that is not 100% wide, and you want to center-align it, set the CSS margin property to auto.


METHOD 1: Aligning DIV horizontally side by side

Origins & History

  • Ancient Persia & Mesopotamia: Early depictions guarding gold and treasures
  • Greek mythology: Sacred to Apollo, associated with the sun and divine power
  • Medieval Europe: Common in coats of arms and architecture as a symbol of protection

Symbolism

Since the eagle is the king of the sky and the lion is the king of the land, the griffin symbolizes power over both air and earth.
They are often shown as guardians of gold, temples, or royal property

In Modern Culture

    Griffins appear in:
  1. Fantasy books & movies (e.g., Harry Potter, The Witcher)
  2. Video games (Dungeons & Dragons)
  3. Logos, emblems, and fantasy art.
NOTE: The CSS float property was not originally meant to align 'div' elements side-by-side, but has been used for this purpose for many years.
CSS float property is used for positioning and formatting content and allows elements to be positioned horizontally, rather than vertically.
